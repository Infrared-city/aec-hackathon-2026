Analysis Summary — Summer Conditions
The site currently exhibits an urban heat island intensity of +4.2°C relative to the rural reference, driven by high surface coverage, limited vegetation, and an elevated sky view factor (SVF 0.91) exposing surfaces to direct solar load throughout the day. Peak surface temperatures reach 36–38°C in July, resulting in UTCI values of 30–34°C during afternoon hours — well beyond the thermal comfort threshold.

Simulated introduction of 18 deciduous trees (Tilia cordata, canopy diameter 6–8 m, placed at 8 m spacing) reduces mean radiant temperature by −6.4°C, brings UTCI into the 22–26°C comfort range for 74% of occupied hours, and provides 38 W/m² of evapotranspirative cooling. Estimated CO₂ sequestration: 12.4 t CO₂/year. Biodiversity index improves from 0.18 to 0.54 (BREEAM baseline reference).
Design strategies & recommendations
01Priority zone: SE corner — highest heat load
SE surfaces reach 36–38°C surface temperature during peak summer afternoon (14:00–17:00 CET). Plant 5 Tilia cordata at 8 m spacing to provide maximum mid-afternoon shading and maximise evapotranspirative cooling contribution. Expected UTCI reduction: −5 to −7°C in this zone.
Thermal comfort · UTCI
02Linear deciduous buffer along the main pedestrian axis
A NW–SE tree alignment along the primary movement corridor creates a continuous shaded pathway that reduces summer solar radiation exposure by 55–70% while preserving winter solar access (sun angle <20°). Species selection: deciduous with crown lift ≥2.5 m for unobstructed passage.
Solar radiation · Shadow analysis
03Biodiverse understory to raise biodiversity score
Beneath the tree canopies, introduce a native shrub and groundcover layer (e.g. Geranium sanguineum, Festuca glauca). This raises the biodiversity index from 0.54 to an estimated 0.72, above the threshold for ecological network connectivity at the neighbourhood scale.
Biodiversity · Air quality
04High-albedo permeable paving in non-planted zones
Apply high-solar-reflectance permeable paving (albedo ≥ 0.40) in the remaining hard-surface areas to reduce stored heat re-radiation. This lowers the evening UTCI by an additional 1–2°C and contributes to stormwater management — reducing runoff by approximately 60% during peak rainfall events.
Solar radiation · Urban heat island
05Green corridor connection to regional network
Align planting strategy with the regional green corridor connecting toward Fælledparken (1.8 km NE). This enables habitat connectivity and allows the urban canopy to function as part of a cooling network rather than isolated interventions, amplifying the urban heat island mitigation effect at district scale.
Climate regulation · Human well-being
